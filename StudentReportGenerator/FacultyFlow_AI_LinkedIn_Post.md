Teachers didn't get into education to spend their evenings writing 30 versions of the same paragraph about "attendance" and "effort."

But that's report season. Every term. In every school.

I built FacultyFlow AI to fix that.

Here's the gap it fills:

Most "AI report writing" right now is a teacher copy-pasting scribbled notes into a generic chatbot and hoping the tone lands right. No structure, no memory of the student, no control, and no clear idea where that child's data actually goes.

FacultyFlow AI isn't a chatbot with a school logo slapped on it. It's built around how a teacher actually writes a report:

📝 Enter attendance, contributions, a curriculum topic and a few notes — get a polished, professional draft in seconds, not an hour
📚 Generate reports for an entire class in one batch, not one painful student at a time
⚖️ Compare how two different AI models write the same report side-by-side, and pick the one that actually sounds like you
🎯 Built-in tone frameworks, pronoun accuracy, and SEN/support-need context, so reports read as individual, not templated
🔒 Every student profile and report is encrypted locally on the teacher's own device. Nothing trains an AI model. Nothing sits on a server it doesn't need to
🧠 Works with whichever provider a school already trusts — OpenAI, Google Gemini, Anthropic's Claude, or a free NVIDIA model — no vendor lock-in

The result isn't an AI-written report. It's a teacher's report, written faster, because they didn't have to start from a blank page.
